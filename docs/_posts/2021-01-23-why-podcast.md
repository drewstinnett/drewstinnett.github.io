I've loved listening to podcasts for a long time.  As long as we've lived in
the RTP area, I've always had a longer than average commute.  Podcasts were a
great way to make that commute enjoyable.  Every day I had a set of morning
drive podcasts for the long drive in, and a set of afternoon drive podcasts for
the trip back home. Podcasts helped to get me in the mood for work on that
drive in, and get me _out_ of the mood for work on the way home.

Personally, I never felt like I had a topic that would be interesting enough or
entertaining enough for other folks to listen to.  No big deal though, I've
always been happy to be a podcast consumer. Then came 2020...work switched to
work-from-home...people everywhere were getting sick...no more going out to
eat...no more movies...and violence towards the Black community was suddenly
highlighted for me.  I don't know if the highlighting had more to do with being
home more, or the killing of George Floyd...or some combination of all of that,
but it was quickly apparent that we were not in a good place (and for many
people, had not been in a good place for a very long time).

The George Floyd killing hit me especially hard...while he obviously was not
the first Black person killed unjustly by police, there was something about
this that felt different.  It was on camera...people were begging the officer
to stop...and it was just so slow...not a moment of bad judgement or an
accidental gun discharge...just an almost 10 minute act of killing another
human being, with face so casual, he could have been ordering a cup of coffee,
instead of choking a person to death.

All of this together got me thinking..."Drew, why are you so blind to this?
This isn't new, why didn't you see it before?".  As a lover of all things
media, I started looking at my world.  Podcasts...hmmm...all these podcasts I
listen to are made by white (mostly male) people.  Music?  Yeah...it's a bunch
of white people.  Movies?  Well, here I've been a little better, but still
mostly white-made-movies. Ok, this will be easy to fix (I naively
thought)...time to start branching out!  I added more diverse podcasts tot he
rotation, like [The Breakfast
Club](https://www.iheart.com/podcast/the-breakfast-club-24992238/) and [Code
Switch](https://www.npr.org/podcasts/510312/codeswitch)...started listening to
Hip-Hop that was made after the Vanilla Ice period...and started revisiting
some of my favorite Spike Lee films. While I expected this to make me feel
better (like it's me feeling better that even matters in all this), but it all
just highlighted more things I'd been completely blind to...it felt bad.

One of my colleagues, Rochelle Newton, started doing [Diversity Chats on
YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCkyawZ1jIpKyagv56kwDCVg/videos), to
get a conversation going between all types of people. She asked me to
participate in [one](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=u4Df9rCfHm8&t=1809s).
Talking felt great, but watching her talk with other folks felt even better.
Seeing and hearing what other people in my community go through on a daily
basis first hand was very eye opening. Rochelle was piping multiple interviews
out every week, with all sorts of incredibly interesting people. She came up
with the idea to make a podcast similar to her YouTube series and asked me to
co-host, which I obviously jumped at the opportunity to do. This is how
[Imminent Teachnology](https://podcast.imminentteachnology.com/) was born.
